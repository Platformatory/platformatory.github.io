<div class="about">
<p><b>About This Series</b><br>
Apache Kafka, Spark, Flink, Iceberg are the state of the art technologies for data engineering. This series focuses on how to use them to build real-time, data-intensive applications.</p>

<p><b>What you will learn</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Essential elements of data infrastructure theory</li>
<li>Hands-on end-to-end streaming data pipelines</li>
</ul>

<p><b>Who This Is For</b><br>
Engineers who are:</p>
<ul>
<li>Working in data engineering, platform engineering, or a related role</li>
<li>Comfortable with the basics and ready to move to intermediate or advanced topics</li>
<li>Interested in understanding system internals, not just APIs and configuration</li>
<li>Looking to build production-grade knowledge in streaming and data infrastructure</li>
</ul>

<p><b>What you will bring</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Laptop: With docker-compose installed. (Fully charged)</li>
<li>Lots of enthusiasm to follow along and learn</li>
</ul>

<p><b>Topics</b></p>
<p>Across all three technologies:</p>
<ul>
<li>Theory grounded in system internals - the reasoning behind architectural decisions</li>
<li>Hands-on labs that participants follow along in real time</li>
<li>Use-cases from fintech, logistics, e-commerce, and analytics</li>
</ul>
